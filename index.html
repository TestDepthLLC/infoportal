Emergency Preparedness

Focus on the big levers first: water, power/heat, communications, and first aid. Start small and build consistency.

  • Water: store what you can, then add treatment (filters, tabs). Use the calculator to plan.
  • Power & heat: light, phone charging, and basic warmth. Think batteries first, then fuel.
  • First aid: IFAK for trauma, plus a household kit. Training beats gear.
  • Evac kits: a simple 72-hour bag per person cuts decision load in a hurry.

Digital Security

Keep conversations private, devices sane, and your footprint small. Start with a simple threat model.

  • Messaging: end-to-end encryption, safety numbers, and backups.
  • Device hygiene: updates, full-disk encryption, strong unlock, trusted app stores.
  • Browser privacy: sane defaults, containers/profiles, and DNS choices.
  • Operational security: minimize metadata; separate identities and tasks.
